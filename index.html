The plan

One path, built in segments

The Riverwalk is a six-foot, hard-surfaced path on micro piles — built piece by piece to navigate the riverbank while protecting habitat. Follow the footsteps down the trail.

Segment 1 · Funded · Building 2026

The Chewuch Bridge underpass

The first segment carries the route under the Chewuch River Bridge — funded and slated for construction this year, taking pedestrians safely beneath a busy crossing.

Segment 2 · Design underway

Susie Stephens Trail to Confluence Park

A 950-foot path from the Susie Stephens Trail to Confluence Park — the stretch that finally lets walkers bypass the Highway 20 fog line. $2M of the $3.5M is already in hand, and landowners have donated the easements.

Along the way

Bank & habitat restoration

Replace failing bank stabilization where the Chewuch meets the Methow, and restore riparian habitat for native fish and wildlife — within and alongside the river channel.

The vision

One continuous Riverwalk

Tie the segments together through Confluence Park and the downtown boardwalk, with interpretive signs and kiosks celebrating the area's natural and human history — a free, all-season path open to everyone.
